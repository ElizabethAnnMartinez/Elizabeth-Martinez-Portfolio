About Me

I'm Elizabeth Martinez, a Mechanical engineer student at Northeastern University pursuing a minor in Aerospace engineering.

I've spent the last several years finding that the best way to learn is to actually create things, from conception to finalization, and then do it all over again to make it better. My start to engineering was through FIRST robotics, where I started as a designed, focusing on prototyping systems and being the person to 'wear many hats' as I had to quickly become skilled on new things as issues arose during the season. Over the course of my four years on the team, I grew to be Head Design Lead as well as Team President where I was running an 80-person team and managing the full lifecycle of robots starting from a drawing on a white board until a competition ready robot. That experience taught me how to make high-stakes decisions fast, integrate between different disciplines (design, electronics, programming, etc.), and lead people who may not know the first thing about engineering.

That drive and passion to grow my skills took me to an internship at NASA, where I designed and manufactured precision parts for a land terrain vehicle where I specifically made industry standard drawings, cut parts on a waterjet, and rewired a drivetrain.

Nowadays I mainly spend my time designing air brakes for a rocket with NU Aerospace, teaching SolidWorks certification courses to 150+ students a semester, and pushing myself into every hands-on opportunity I can find. Whether it's learning TIG welding or creating a smart trash can that helps people with limited mobility.
